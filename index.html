Research Focus

My doctoral work at Princeton centers on developing manifold-based turbulence-chemistry interaction models for supersonic and hypersonic combustion. I work within the Computational Turbulent Reacting Flow Laboratory under Prof. Michael E. Mueller, targeting propulsion applications including rotating detonation engines and scramjets.

Technical Background

I build and deploy large-scale scientific software in C++, Fortran, and Python on HPC clusters. I have hands-on experience with GPU acceleration (OpenACC), parallel computing (MPI/OpenMP), and physics-constrained machine learning (PyTorch) from my internship at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory.
