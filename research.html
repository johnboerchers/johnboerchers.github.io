Research

My research develops computational methods for high-speed turbulent reacting flows, with applications in hypersonic propulsion, rotating detonation engines, and scramjets. A central theme is building reduced-order models that compress complex, high-dimensional physical systems into efficient representations — bridging rigorous mathematical formulation with practical large-scale software.

Experience
Princeton
2022 — Present

Computational Turbulent Reacting Flow Laboratory

Graduate Research Assistant  ·  Advisor: Prof. Michael E. Mueller

Developing manifold-based turbulence-chemistry interaction models that extend large eddy simulation (LES) capability to premixed deflagrations and detonations. This work addresses a critical gap in existing reduced-order approaches: accurately capturing thermodynamic consistency and enthalpy variations in high-speed reacting flows.

Implementing and validating models within PeleC, an AMR-enabled exascale reacting flow solver. Work involves designing numerical algorithms, building HPC workflows, and benchmarking against direct numerical simulation (DNS) data in collaboration with NREL.

LLNL
June — Aug 2022

Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory

Research Intern  ·  Advisor: Boyan Lazarov

Built a physics-constrained neural network in PyTorch, trained on direct numerical simulation data, to serve as a surrogate model for topology optimization of porous-media flow problems. Enforced physical conservation laws as hard constraints during training to improve generalizability beyond the training distribution.
